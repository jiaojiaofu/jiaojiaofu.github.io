    Jiaojiao Fu

    Assistant Professor, ECUST

    • Xuhui, Shanghai
    • Email
    • Google Scholar

    I am a Lecturer (Assistant Professor) in the School of Information Science and Engineering at East China University of Science and Technology. I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from Fudan University. Prior to this, I served as an Assistant Researcher at the Shenzhen Research Institute, The Chinese University of Hong Kong. I hold an M.Sc. from Peking University and a B.Sc. from Qufu Normal University.

    My research focuses on leveraging computer technology to tackle real-world challenges, enhance the societal impact of technology, and promote well-being through technological innovation. I adopt a user-centered approach, emphasizing a deep understanding of user needs, developing tailored solutions to enhance the overall user experience.

    I have applied user research methodologies to explore the application needs of information systems in chronic disease management scenarios. By leveraging natural language processing (NLP) and large language model technologies, I have worked to enhance the efficiency and quality of disease diagnosis, treatment, and management. Additionally, I have long focused on the difficulties faced by the elderly in using technology, investigating innovative solutions such as voice interaction and automation to help older adults better integrate into digital life. Overall, my research centers on the application of large language models (LLM) and spans the fields of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and Computer-Supported Cooperative Work (CSCW), ensuring that technological products can better meet user needs.

    Please feel free to contact me if you are interested in my research.
