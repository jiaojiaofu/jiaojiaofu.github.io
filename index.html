    Jiaojiao Fu

    Assistant Professor, ECUST

    • Xuhui, Shanghai
    • Email
    • Google Scholar

    I am a Lecturer (Assistant Professor) in the School of Information Science and Engineering at East China University of Science and Technology. I received my Ph.D. in Computer Science from Fudan University. Prior to this, I served as an Assistant Researcher at the Shenzhen Research Institute, The Chinese University of Hong Kong. I hold an M.Sc. from Peking University and a B.Sc. from Qufu Normal University.

    My research focuses on leveraging computer technology to tackle real-world challenges, enhance the societal impact of technology, and promote well-being through technological innovation. I adopt a user-centered approach, emphasizing a deep understanding of user needs, developing tailored solutions, and improving software reliability to enhance the overall user experience.

    I have applied user research methodologies to explore the application requirements of information systems in managing chronic diseases. By harnessing natural language processing (NLP) and big data analytics, my goal is to improve the efficiency and quality of chronic disease diagnosis and treatment. I am also committed to addressing the challenges that older adults face when interacting with technology. My work explores innovative solutions, such as voice interaction and automated technologies, to facilitate their engagement with digital tools. Additionally, I employ techniques like log analysis and mobile computing to enhance software reliability, with a particular focus on security and performance, ensuring that the software meets users' needs more effectively. Overall, my research focuses on the fields of Human-Computer Interaction (HCI), Computer-Supported Cooperative Work (CSCW), and Software Engineering (SE).

    Please feel free to contact me if you are interested in my research.
